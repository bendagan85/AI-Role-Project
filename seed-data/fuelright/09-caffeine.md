# Caffeine and Performance

General performance nutrition. Not medical advice. If you have heart conditions, anxiety disorders, are pregnant, or take medications, talk to a clinician before adjusting caffeine.

## What caffeine does

Caffeine blocks adenosine receptors. Adenosine is the molecule that builds up through the day and makes you sleepy. Blocking it makes you feel more awake.

In the gym, caffeine:
- Increases strength output by ~3–7%
- Improves endurance by ~5–10%
- Reduces perceived exertion
- Improves focus and reaction time

It's one of the few supplements with massive amounts of replicated research showing real ergogenic effects.

## How much

The performance-enhancing dose is **3–6 mg per kg of bodyweight**, taken 30–45 minutes before training.

For a 75 kg adult: 225–450 mg.

Reference points:
- 1 cup of brewed coffee: 80–120 mg
- 1 shot of espresso: 60–80 mg
- 1 can of energy drink: 80–300 mg (read the label)
- Pre-workout supplement: typically 150–300 mg

Doses above 6 mg/kg show diminishing returns and increased side effects.

## Timing

- **Pre-workout:** 30–45 minutes before training
- **Half-life:** 5–6 hours typically, longer in some genetic variants
- **Cutoff:** 8 hours before bedtime if you want unaffected sleep

If you sleep at 10 PM, last caffeine at 2 PM. Period. Many people who claim "caffeine doesn't affect my sleep" haven't actually compared their sleep quality with and without late caffeine. Sleep tracker data consistently shows it does.

## Tolerance and cycling

Tolerance builds in 1–2 weeks of daily use. You'll still feel the wakefulness, but the ergogenic effect on performance diminishes.

Strategies:
- **Lower habitual intake.** If your baseline is 600 mg/day, dropping to 200 mg/day will make pre-workout caffeine work better.
- **Cycle:** 1 week off every 6–8 weeks. The first 3–4 days are unpleasant. Then your sensitivity resets.
- **Use it only for important sessions:** If you only take caffeine 3–4 days a week, you maintain higher sensitivity.

## Genetic variation

Some people metabolize caffeine fast (CYP1A2 fast metabolizer); some slow. Slow metabolizers:
- Feel jittery on smaller doses
- Sleep poorly even on early-day caffeine
- May have slightly increased cardiovascular risk from high intakes

You can do a genetic test, but most people figure out their tolerance through experience. If 200 mg makes you anxious or sleepless, you're probably a slow metabolizer.

## Side effects to watch

- **Sleep disruption:** Even if you fall asleep fine, late caffeine reduces deep sleep quality.
- **Anxiety:** Caffeine amplifies anxiety in susceptible individuals.
- **Blood pressure:** Acute spikes during caffeine intake. Concerning if you have hypertension.
- **GI distress:** Some people get diarrhea from caffeine, especially with low food intake.
- **Tolerance / dependence:** Withdrawal headaches when you skip a day.

## Caffeine and cutting

During a calorie deficit, caffeine has additional benefits:
- Modestly suppresses appetite
- Maintains training quality despite lower energy availability
- Small thermogenic effect (50–100 kcal/day, not magic)

Don't rely on caffeine to fix a poorly-designed cut. But it's a useful tool.

## What about energy drinks?

The caffeine is the active ingredient. Most other ingredients (taurine, ginseng, B-vitamins) have minimal effect at the doses in energy drinks.

If you like energy drinks, pick low-sugar versions and treat them as caffeine delivery. Don't get more than ~300 mg in a single drink.

## Practical patterns

- **Daily coffee drinker:** 1–2 cups in the morning, plus 200 mg pre-workout. Cut off by 2 PM.
- **Pre-workout only:** 200–400 mg, 30 min before training, 3–4x per week.
- **Cyclical:** 6 weeks of regular intake, 1 week off, repeat.
- **Slow metabolizer:** 100–150 mg max, only in the morning.

## What to skip

- Multi-stim pre-workouts mixing caffeine with other stimulants like DMHA, yohimbine, synephrine. Increased side effects without proportional benefits.
- Caffeine after lunch if you care about sleep.
- "Detox" claims around caffeine. Just ramp down over a week.
