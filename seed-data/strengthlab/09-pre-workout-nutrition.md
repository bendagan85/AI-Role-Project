# Pre-Workout Nutrition Basics for Lifters

Note: this is the strength side of the conversation. For the nutrition deep-dive (macros, TDEE, supplements), check the FuelRight knowledge base — they share some terminology with us but go much further.

## The simple version

If you've eaten reasonably well across the day, a heavy lifting session 2–3 hours after your last full meal is fine. You don't need to eat 30 minutes before training. You don't need a pre-workout shake. You don't need caffeine.

What you DO need:
- Adequate carbohydrate availability (your muscles are stocked with glycogen).
- Reasonable hydration.
- Not eating a giant meal in the 60 minutes before lifting.

## Carbs matter for strength training

Pure strength sets (1–5 reps) are short and don't actually deplete much glycogen. But hypertrophy work and long sessions (60+ min) burn through it. Going into a hard session glycogen-depleted means worse working sets, especially in the second half.

For most lifters: 3–5 g/kg of bodyweight per day of carbs supports normal training. Time of intake matters less than total daily intake. If you're training fasted in the morning, you'll be relying on liver glycogen, which is finite — be ready for the bar to feel heavier.

## Protein around training

The "anabolic window" was overhyped. Total daily protein matters far more than timing. Aim for 1.6–2.2 g/kg/day of protein across 3–5 evenly-spaced meals. If you're hitting that, your "post-workout protein" is just whatever meal happens next.

That said, the meal closest to your workout (within 2–3 hours after) should include 30–50g of protein. This is more about hitting daily total than about magic timing.

## Pre-workout caffeine

Useful, especially for early-morning training or if you didn't sleep well. 3–6 mg/kg of bodyweight is the ergogenic dose. For a 75 kg lifter that's 225–450 mg.

Timing: 30–45 minutes before training.

Side effects to know:
- Increased blood pressure during sets — fine if you're healthy, worth noting if you have BP issues.
- Diminished returns above 6 mg/kg.
- Tolerance builds. Cycle off periodically.

## What to skip

- "Pre-workout" powders with proprietary blends. They're caffeine + filler + marketing.
- Fasted heavy training as a default. It works for some, but most lifters will hit harder PRs after a small carb-heavy snack 60 minutes out.
- New supplements on heavy training days. Test them on low-stakes sessions first.

## Practical defaults

- **Training within 90 min of waking:** small mixed snack (banana + whey, ~30g carb / 20g protein).
- **Training mid-day:** last full meal 2–3 hours out. No need for a pre-workout snack.
- **Training in the evening:** last meal 1.5–2 hours out, with some carbs.

## Hydration

Don't show up dehydrated. 500 mL of water in the 1–2 hours before lifting. Sip during. Avoid chugging 1 liter in the 20 minutes before lifting — it'll just sit in your stomach.

If you sweat heavily or train in heat, electrolytes (especially sodium) are worth attention. Most lifters who feel "weak" during long sessions are under-sodium, not under-water.
